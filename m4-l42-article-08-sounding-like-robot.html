The Risk of Sounding Like a Robot: Keeping Your Brand Human

AI can write fast, but it has a signature style problem — unedited AI content often sounds clinical, hollow, and fake. Here's how to spot it and fix it.

There's a growing problem in social media marketing: content that looks human but reads like it was generated by a machine. Smooth sentences, technically correct, no obvious errors — but something feels off. AI-generated content, when published without human editing, tends to overuse certain phrases, lack specific detail, avoid genuine opinion, and produce language that sounds like it was designed to please everyone — which usually resonates with no one.

Audiences are getting better at detecting this — and when they do, trust erodes. The solution isn't to use less AI. It's to know what AI gets wrong and fix it before publishing.

The AI Writing "Tells" — Red Flags to Look For

🚩
Hollow enthusiasm phrases
"We're excited to announce..." · "We are thrilled to share..." · "We're passionate about delivering..." — these sound copy-pasted because they are. AI defaults to enthusiasm words that feel empty.
🚩
Corporate buzzword stacking
"Our innovative, best-in-class solution leverages cutting-edge technology to deliver seamless experiences..." — AI loves stacking adjectives and buzzwords, which are universally disliked.
🚩
No specific details or opinions
AI rarely includes specific numbers, names, or genuine opinions. "Our coffee is really good" vs. "Every cup we roast is pulled at exactly 200°F — three degrees hotter than industry standard." Specificity is what AI avoids.
🚩
Perfect parallel structure in lists
When every sentence in a list is the same length, starts the same way, and ends symmetrically — that's AI formatting. Humans write messier, more varied sentences.

The Human Edit Checklist

Before publishing any AI-generated post, ask: (1) Does this sound like a specific person or a generic company? (2) Is there at least one concrete detail AI couldn't have invented? (3) Does it express an actual opinion or take a position? (4) Would I cringe if someone asked "did AI write this?" If the answer to any of these is wrong, it needs another human pass.

📌 What You Just Learned

Article Summary

Unedited AI content has recognizable "tells" — hollow enthusiasm phrases, buzzword stacking, lack of specifics, and perfect parallel structure that no human writes naturally.

Audiences are increasingly able to detect AI-generated content — and when they do, trust in the brand decreases. Human editing is not optional.

The fix is adding specificity, genuine opinion, and authentic voice — things only a human who actually knows the brand can provide.

A simple checklist — does this sound human, does it have a real detail, does it take a position — catches most AI quality issues before publishing.
