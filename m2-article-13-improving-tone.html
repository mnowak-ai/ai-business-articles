How to Improve Tone in AI-Generated Writing

AI has two default tone failures: overly enthusiastic and robotically formal. Both are recognizable to any experienced reader — and fixing them is one of the most important editing skills you can develop.

You can always tell when something was written by AI without being edited. Not because of grammar errors — AI's grammar is usually fine. It's the tone. Either it sounds like an overeager assistant who's "absolutely delighted" about everything, or it sounds like a legal document that's been translated from corporate robot into slightly warmer corporate robot.

Neither is what you want. The goal is writing that sounds like a competent, thoughtful professional who actually knows and respects the recipient — and that requires human calibration of tone. Here's how to recognize and fix the most common patterns.

The Six Most Common AI Tone Problems

These patterns show up consistently in AI-generated writing. Each one has a recognizable signature and a straightforward fix.

😄
Unearned Enthusiasm
Very Common
❌ AI default
"I am absolutely thrilled and delighted to connect with you today and share this incredible opportunity that I am confident will transform your operations!"
✅ Fixed
"I wanted to share an opportunity I think could be a strong fit for your team. Here's the overview."
🤖
Robotic Formality
Very Common
❌ AI default
"Please be advised that the aforementioned documentation has been received and is currently under review by the relevant stakeholders."
✅ Fixed
"Got your documents — our team is reviewing them now and will follow up by Friday."
😤
Hidden Passive-Aggression
Common
❌ AI default
"As per our previous communication, and as I noted in my last email, the deadline was clearly stated as the 15th of this month."
✅ Fixed
"Just a quick reminder that the deadline is the 15th — let me know if you need anything before then."
🫥
Empty Filler Phrases
Very Common
❌ AI default
"I hope this email finds you well. Don't hesitate to reach out if you have any questions or concerns. Looking forward to connecting."
✅ Fixed
Cut all three. Start with your actual message. End with your specific ask. Nothing else needed.
🏢
Corporate Buzzword Overload
Common
❌ AI default
"We are excited to synergize our core competencies to leverage best-in-class solutions that will move the needle on your key performance indicators going forward."
✅ Fixed
"We can help your team work more efficiently and hit your targets faster. Here's how."
🎭
Tone Mismatch for Context
Common
❌ Wrong context
Upbeat, celebratory tone used in an email addressing a client complaint: "Exciting news — we've had a chance to review your feedback!"
✅ Right context
"Thank you for letting us know about this. I understand this wasn't the experience you expected, and I want to make sure we address it properly."

The fastest way to improve AI tone is to ask: "Would a real human I respect actually say this?" If the answer is no, rewrite it until the answer is yes.

The Ultimate Tone Test

Before sending any AI-assisted document, read it aloud. Your ear catches what your eyes skip over. If you stumble on a phrase, it probably sounds unnatural. If you feel slightly embarrassed reading something out loud — "absolutely delighted," "please don't hesitate" — that's your instinct telling you it needs to go. Trust that instinct.

📌 Article Summary

What You Just Learned

AI has two default tone failures: unearned enthusiasm (too effusive) and robotic formality (too stiff). Both are recognizable and fixable.

Six common AI tone problems: unearned enthusiasm, robotic formality, hidden passive-aggression, empty filler phrases, corporate buzzwords, and context mismatches.

Many tone problems can be fixed with specific refinement prompts that tell the AI exactly what to change — no need to rewrite from scratch.

The ultimate tone test: read your document aloud. If you feel embarrassed saying it, it needs to be rewritten. Trust your instinct.
