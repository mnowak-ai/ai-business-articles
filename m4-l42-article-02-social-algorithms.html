How Social Media Algorithms Decide What You See

Nobody sees everything posted on social media. Algorithms decide what gets shown — and what gets buried. Understanding how they work is the key to creating content that actually reaches people.

Every social media platform has billions of pieces of content and hundreds of millions of users. There's no way every user could see every post — so each platform uses an algorithm to decide what to show each person in what order. These algorithms are essentially ranking systems — they score every piece of content based on dozens of signals, then show you the highest-scoring content first.

For marketers, this means that posting great content is only the first step. You also need to understand how the algorithm scores content — so your posts earn enough ranking points to actually be seen by the people you're trying to reach.

What Every Algorithm Cares About Most

Every platform's algorithm is different, but they all optimize for the same thing: keeping users on the platform as long as possible. Content that makes people stop scrolling, engage, and come back is content the algorithm rewards.

✅ Algorithm Rewards
High completion rates · Saves · Shares · Comments · Meaningful engagement · Watch time · Quick replies · Profile visits after seeing post
❌ Algorithm Penalizes
Immediate scrolling past · Low watch time · No engagement · Muted accounts · Reported content · Outbound links that take users off-platform · Repetitive posting

The Organic Reach Reality Check

Average organic reach (the % of your followers who actually see a given post) has declined significantly on most platforms. This is partly algorithmic and partly because competition for feed space has exploded.

Facebook
1–3%
average organic reach of page followers per post (down from 16% in 2012)
Instagram
3–6%
average organic reach for feed posts from business accounts
TikTok
Up to 100%
new account posts can reach non-followers if content performs well (For You Page)

TikTok's radical difference is why it became so important for brands — its algorithm gives new and small accounts the same shot at viral reach as established accounts, purely based on content performance.

The Marketer's Algorithm Playbook

Smart marketers don't fight the algorithm — they work with it. This means: posting consistently (algorithms favor active accounts), using formats each platform prioritizes (Reels on Instagram, carousels on LinkedIn), generating saves and shares rather than just likes (higher-value signals), posting when your audience is most active, and always encouraging comments with questions or prompts. Understanding the algorithm isn't gaming the system — it's speaking the platform's language.

📌 What You Just Learned

Article Summary

Social media algorithms rank content based on engagement signals — saves, shares, comments, watch time — with the goal of keeping users on the platform as long as possible.

Each platform weights signals differently: TikTok prioritizes watch time and completion rate; Instagram rewards saves and Reels; LinkedIn boosts content that keeps users on-platform.

Organic reach has declined sharply on most platforms — posting isn't enough. You need content that earns algorithmic distribution through strong engagement signals.

Working with the algorithm — using preferred formats, posting consistently, encouraging high-value signals like saves and comments — is how modern marketers earn free reach.
