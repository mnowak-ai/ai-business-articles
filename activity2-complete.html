📄 FutureMove Athletics — Strategic Investment Analysis

INTERNAL BRIEF · CONFIDENTIAL

Company Background

FutureMove Athletics is a growing athletic footwear company selling running and training shoes primarily online. Current profile:

3 physical retail stores
25 customer service employees
$5M available to invest
0 advanced AI systems in place

Most sales happen through the company website. The company must decide how to allocate its investment budget across four options.

Industry & Market Data

Online vs. In-Store Shopping Trends

72% of athletic shoe purchases happen online
12%/yr online sales growth
3%/yr in-store sales growth

Customer Preferences

  • 🌱68% of Gen Z prefer sustainable (eco-friendly) products — a growing segment that directly impacts brand loyalty
  • 🎯70% of online shoppers are more likely to buy when given personalized product recommendations
  • 📱80% of Gen Z discover brands through social media — meaning digital presence drives top-of-funnel growth

Competitive Landscape

Major competitors (Nike, Adidas, New Balance) already use AI for product recommendations, offer fast digital customer service, and invest heavily in online marketing. FutureMove must stay competitive or risk losing market share to better-equipped rivals.

Investment Options & Financial Analysis

Option Setup Cost Annual Cost Expected Revenue Impact Key Risk
🤖 AI Customer Service $800,000 $150,000/yr maintenance +10% repeat purchases; saves 30–40% on $1M labor costs Some layoffs; customers may prefer humans
🏪 Open 10 New Stores $3,000,000 $200,000/yr per store $500K/yr per store; $50K–$75K profit per store Mall foot traffic down 15% over 5 years
🎯 AI Product Recommendations $1,200,000 Lower maintenance +15–25% online sales; ~$1M additional yearly revenue Technology implementation challenges
🌱 Sustainable Product Line $1,000,000 Higher production costs ongoing Brand loyalty + potential pricing premium Higher production costs; slower revenue impact
🤖 AI Customer Service — Deeper Look
  • Current labor: 25 employees, $1M/yr in salaries
  • AI setup: $800K one-time, $150K/yr maintenance
  • Handles 60% of customer questions automatically
  • Reduces labor costs by 30–40%
  • Faster response times = better experience
  • +10% increase in repeat purchase rate
✓ Strong ROI potential ⚠ Layoff risk + human preference
🏪 10 New Retail Stores — Deeper Look
  • Total setup: $3M across 10 stores
  • Operating cost: $200K/yr per store ($2M/yr total)
  • Expected revenue: $500K/yr per store ($5M/yr)
  • Profit per store: only $50K–$75K annually
  • In-store growth rate: just 3%/yr vs 12% online
  • Mall foot traffic down 15% over the past 5 years
✓ Brand visibility gains ⚠ Thin margins, declining foot traffic

Possible Combination Strategies (within $5M budget)

Because the total budget is $5M, multiple options can be combined if individual costs allow. Two strong combinations:

CombinationTotal CostStrategic Rationale
🤖 AI Customer Service + 🎯 AI Recommendations $2,000,000 Full digital transformation — automate support AND personalize shopping. Matches competitor capabilities.
🎯 AI Recommendations + 🌱 Sustainable Line $2,200,000 Drive online sales growth while building brand loyalty with Gen Z — the fastest-growing customer segment.

Key Strategic Considerations for Decision-Makers

  • 📊Online shopping is growing 4x faster than in-store (12% vs 3%). A company with most of its sales online should prioritize digital infrastructure.
  • 🤖AI tools — especially product recommendations — have a demonstrated ROI: 70% of online shoppers buy more when recommendations are personalized.
  • 🏪Retail expansion carries high fixed costs ($200K/yr per store) with thin margins ($50–75K profit per store). This approach fights an uphill battle against declining foot traffic.
  • 🌱Sustainability investments may not generate immediate revenue but create long-term loyalty with Gen Z — the generation entering peak spending years.
  • ⚖️Any AI investment involving workforce reduction requires careful change management. Customer perception of automated service varies significantly by segment.
💡
How to use this brief: Refer back to it in every section. When AI gives you a response, check it against these numbers. If AI makes a claim that contradicts or ignores this data, that's a gap worth noting.

SECTION 03 OF 06

Push AI Into Decision Mode

Your first prompt asked AI to summarize. Now you're going to force it to take a stand — with context, trade-offs, and a real recommendation.

⏱ 10–12 minutes
1

Upgrade Your Prompt

~5–6 min

A decision-forcing prompt does three things:

🎯 Forces one recommendation
🏢 Includes company-specific context
⚖️ Demands trade-offs and risks

See the difference:

❌ Weak Prompt "What are the pros and cons of AI automation vs retail expansion?"
✅ Strong Prompt "You're advising the CEO of StrideX — a company where 72% of sales are online, customer satisfaction is falling, and competitors already use AI. Recommend ONE option — AI automation or retail expansion — and explain the trade-offs."
"You are advising the CEO of a global retail company deciding between investing in AI-powered customer service automation or expanding physical stores. Company context: StrideX Global — 3,200 employees, 42 countries, $10M investment budget. Customer satisfaction scores are declining. Competitors are investing heavily in digital. 72% of sales happen online and are growing at 12%/yr. In-store sales grow at only 3%/yr. Your task: Recommend ONE option. Justify your choice using cost, revenue potential, risk, and strategic fit. Explain what could go wrong with your recommendation."

Before running it — verify your prompt does all of this:

  • Forces the AI to pick ONE option (no hedging)
  • Includes StrideX's specific financial or market context
  • Mentions the declining customer satisfaction
  • References the competitive pressure
  • Asks for at least one risk or downside of the recommendation

SECTION 05 OF 06

Executive Decision Brief

This is your final deliverable. One page. CEO-ready. Evidence-based. No "it depends."

⏱ 15–20 minutes

🧾 What Makes a Good Executive Brief?

A CEO doesn't have time for lengthy analysis. They need: the recommendation, the reasons, the risks, and honest caveats — all on one page. Every sentence should earn its place.

Use data from the Research Brief. Reference specific numbers. Name the trade-offs directly. Show you understand what you're recommending — and what you're giving up.

✍️ Executive Writing Rules

  • Lead with the recommendation — don't bury it at the end
  • Use numbers wherever possible ("$200K/yr per store" beats "expensive")
  • Acknowledge the opposing argument — then explain why you still recommend your option
  • Never write "in today's fast-paced world..." — just say what you mean
