🧠 Why Business Email Tone Matters

In a professional setting, your email is often the first — or only — impression a client has of you. Casual greetings, vague timelines, and over-the-top expressions can make you appear unprepared or unprofessional, even when your intentions are good. Professionalism, clarity, and tone work together: a clear email that sounds unprofessional still fails, and a formal email that confuses the reader still fails. All three need to work at once.
