First — What Is a CRM?

CRM stands for Customer Relationship Management. A CRM is software that helps businesses keep track of every interaction they've ever had with every customer or potential customer — all in one place.

Think about what happens when a company has 500 customers and a sales team of 10 people. How do you make sure everyone knows what was promised to which client? How do you know which potential customers are close to buying and which need more attention? How does a new salesperson pick up where the last one left off when a customer calls? Without a system, this becomes chaos.

Vocabulary Worth Knowing

These terms come up constantly in sales and marketing conversations. Learn them now and you'll sound like you've been in the industry for years.

Key Business Vocabulary
CRM

Customer Relationship Management. Software that tracks every interaction between a business and its customers. Salesforce, HubSpot, and Zoho are the big names.

Lead

A potential customer who has shown some interest in your product but hasn't bought yet. "Leads" are what sales teams work to convert into paying customers.

Pipeline

The visual representation of all the deals currently in progress — from first contact to closed sale. "Pipeline value" means how much revenue could come in if all current deals close.

Lead Scoring

Assigning a numerical score to each potential customer based on how likely they are to buy. Higher score = warmer lead = call them first. AI makes this far more accurate than human guesswork.

Conversion Rate

The percentage of leads who end up becoming paying customers. If 100 people visit your product page and 3 buy, your conversion rate is 3%. AI helps improve this by targeting the right people with the right message at the right time.

Forecasting

Predicting how much revenue a sales team will generate in a given period. Accurate forecasting is critical for planning hiring, inventory, and spending. AI makes forecasts significantly more reliable.
