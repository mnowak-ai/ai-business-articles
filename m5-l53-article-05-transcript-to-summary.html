Lesson 5.3 · Meeting Notes & Summaries

From Transcript to Summary: Making AI Work for You

A raw transcript is 5,000 words. A good summary is 200. The gap between those two documents is where human skill — and smart prompting — makes all the difference. This article teaches you how to get from one to the other.

The Gap Between Transcript and Summary

When an AI meeting tool produces a raw transcript, the work isn't done — it's just begun. A 60-minute meeting produces roughly 8,000–10,000 words of transcript. Nobody is going to read that. The value of AI meeting tools isn't the transcript itself; it's the structured summary extracted from the transcript.

That extraction can happen automatically (built-in AI summarization), semi-automatically (you prompt an LLM with the transcript), or manually (you read the transcript and write the summary yourself). In practice, the best workflow combines all three: AI produces a draft, you prompt for specific refinements, and you apply your human judgment to verify accuracy.

Three Types of Summaries — Choose the Right One

⚡
Action-Focused
Only tasks, owners, and deadlines. No discussion context. Perfect for people who were in the meeting and just need the to-do list.
Use when: Sending to attendees post-meeting
📄
Decision Record
Decisions made, who made them, and brief rationale. No individual discussion attribution. Best as a formal record for stakeholders not present.
Use when: Sharing with leadership or clients
📰
Full Recap
Discussion context + decisions + action items + open questions. The complete picture for people who weren't in the room.
Use when: Onboarding someone mid-project, or for high-stakes meetings

Getting Better Summaries: Prompt Tips

When using ChatGPT, Claude, or a built-in AI tool to summarize a transcript, the quality of your output depends heavily on how you frame the request. These additions consistently improve results:

🎯
Specify the audience

"Summarize this for executives who were not present and need only decisions and next steps — no discussion context."

📝
Specify the format

"Format action items as: [Owner]: [Task] → Due [date]. Format decisions as: ✅ DECIDED: [what] — [who]."

⚠️
Ask for flags

"Flag any action items where the commitment seems tentative or the deadline is unclear — I'll review those manually."

🔍
Constrain length

"Keep the discussion summary to 3 sentences maximum. I only need enough context to understand why each decision was made."

A great AI-generated summary and a poor one often come from the exact same transcript. The difference is how well the person reviewing it understands what they're looking for.

📌 Article Summary

What to Remember

Three summary types serve different needs: Action-Focused (to-do list for attendees), Decision Record (formal record for stakeholders), Full Recap (complete context for people who weren't there).

Better AI summaries come from better prompts. Specify audience, format, length constraints, and ask AI to flag uncertain commitments for human review.

Match summary type to audience needs — not more comprehensive than necessary. Sending a 2,000-word full recap when someone needed 3 decisions is poor communication, not thoroughness.
