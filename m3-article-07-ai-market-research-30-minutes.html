Lesson 3.2 · Using AI for Research

How to Use AI to Research a Market in Under 30 Minutes

Market research used to take weeks, cost thousands of dollars, and require a dedicated analyst. With AI, you can produce a solid market overview in a single focused session — if you know the right sequence of questions to ask.

Before AI, building a market research brief meant hiring a research firm, waiting weeks for results, and paying anywhere from $5,000 to $50,000 for a report. That reality locked good research behind big budgets. Small companies, early-stage teams, and individual contributors couldn't afford it.

That has changed dramatically. A well-structured AI research session can produce a market overview that would have taken a junior analyst two weeks — in about 30 minutes. Not because AI knows everything (it doesn't), but because it can rapidly synthesize publicly available knowledge and surface the patterns and benchmarks you need to move forward.

The key word is "structured." Typing "tell me about the coffee market" into an AI tool will produce something generic and shallow. Following the 5-phase framework below will produce something genuinely useful.

The 5-Phase AI Market Research Framework

5 targeted prompt phases. Each one builds on the last. Follow the sequence and you'll have a research brief ready to present.

The Full Walkthrough

Here's each phase in detail — what to ask, why it matters, and what a good AI response looks like. We'll use a real example throughout: researching the online fitness app market for a startup deciding whether to build a new product.

🗺️
Phase 1 · Minutes 0–6
Market Size & Growth

Start with the big picture. You need to know the market's total size (usually measured in dollars per year) and whether it's growing, shrinking, or flat. This tells you if there's a viable opportunity before anything else.

📋 Prompt to use
"Give me an overview of the [online fitness app market]. Include: current market size in USD, annual growth rate, projected size in 5 years, and the 2–3 biggest factors driving that growth. Keep it concise and cite approximate figures."

✅ What good looks like: AI returns specific numbers ($X billion market, X% CAGR), clear growth drivers (like "rising demand for home workouts post-pandemic"), and a projection. Red flag: vague language like "the market is large and growing" with no figures.

🏆
Phase 2 · Minutes 6–12
Competitive Landscape

Now zoom in on who the main players are. You want to understand the competitive structure — is this market dominated by one giant, or fragmented across many players? Who has the most market share and why?

📋 Prompt to use
"Who are the top 5 competitors in the [online fitness app market]? For each one, tell me: their approximate market position, what they do best, and one major weakness or gap in their offering."

✅ What good looks like: A structured list of real companies (Peloton, Noom, MyFitnessPal, etc.) with differentiated strengths and honest weaknesses. Red flag: generic descriptions that could apply to any company in any industry.

👥
Phase 3 · Minutes 12–18
Customer Segments

Understanding who buys in this market is essential. Different customer segments often have completely different needs — and the most profitable segment isn't always the most obvious one.

📋 Prompt to use
"Describe the main customer segments in the [online fitness app market]. For each segment, describe: their demographics, primary motivation for using an app, and what they're most willing to pay for."

✅ What good looks like: 3–5 distinct segments (e.g., casual exercisers, serious athletes, seniors, weight loss seekers) with specific motivational differences. Red flag: one vague "health-conscious consumer" description that covers everyone.

🌊
Phase 4 · Minutes 18–24
Key Trends & Opportunities

This is where you look forward — what's changing in the market right now, and where are the gaps that a new entrant or product could fill?

📋 Prompt to use
"What are the 3 most important trends currently shaping the [online fitness app market]? For each trend, explain what's driving it and what opportunity it creates for a new entrant."

✅ What good looks like: Specific, named trends (e.g., AI-personalized training, wearable integration, social accountability features) with a clear "so what for a new business" statement for each. Red flag: trends so broad they apply to every market (e.g., "digital transformation").

📋
Phase 5 · Minutes 24–30
Summary Brief

The final phase turns everything into a single, presenter-ready brief. This is what you'd share with a manager, investor, or teammate who needs the essential picture without reading the full research session.

📋 Prompt to use
"Based on everything we've discussed about the [online fitness app market], write a 1-page market brief I could present to a leadership team. Include: market size and growth, top 3 competitors, key customer segment, biggest opportunity, and top risk."

✅ What good looks like: A structured, skimmable brief that a non-expert can read in 2 minutes and understand the market opportunity. This is your deliverable — the thing you verify, refine, and present.

How Long This Used to Take vs. Now

⏰ Before AI
2–3 Weeks

A junior analyst manually reading industry reports, building spreadsheets, and writing a market brief from scratch. Cost: $3,000–$15,000 in analyst time or research subscriptions.

⚡ With AI
25–30 Min

One person, one AI tool, five focused prompts. Cost: $0–$20/month for a pro subscription. The output still needs verification — but the starting point is dramatically faster.

This doesn't mean AI replaces analysts. It means analysts — and anyone who learns this framework — can do in 30 minutes what previously required days. The person who knows how to use AI for research has a genuine productivity advantage over the person who doesn't.

⚠️

Always Verify Before You Present

AI market research is an excellent starting point — not a final answer. AI can produce outdated figures, confuse similar companies, or state things with confidence that aren't quite accurate. Before presenting any AI-generated market data, verify at least the key numbers against a second source (a company's own reports, a news article, or a credible industry publication). This takes 5 minutes and protects your credibility completely.

📌 Article Summary

What You Just Learned

The 5-phase AI market research framework covers market size, competitive landscape, customer segments, key trends, and a summary brief — in about 30 minutes.

Structured, specific prompts always outperform vague, broad questions. Each phase builds on the previous one — sequence matters.

AI has compressed market research from weeks of analyst time into a single focused session — but always verify key numbers before presenting to others.

The final Phase 5 brief is your deliverable — a 1-page summary ready to share with any stakeholder who needs the essential market picture without the full research session.
