Lesson 5.2 · Workflow Planning with AI

Tool Spotlight: Asana, Monday.com, and Notion AI

Understanding AI workflow concepts is half the picture. The other half is knowing which tools exist, what they actually do, who uses them, and how to choose between them when you enter a workplace — or build something of your own.

Why the Tool Choice Matters

These three platforms — Asana, Monday.com, and Notion AI — each take a distinct approach to workflow management. All three have added AI features in recent years. But they're not interchangeable: each was built with a different primary user in mind, and each excels in different scenarios.

In most companies you'll work at, one of these tools (or something similar) will already be in use. Understanding what each does well means you can onboard faster, contribute immediately, and make smarter recommendations if you're ever asked to help a team choose or improve their workflow systems.

The Three Tools — Explore Each

🟣
Asana
Work management for structured teams and complex projects

Asana is one of the most widely adopted project management platforms, used by over 130,000 organizations. It's built around a clear task hierarchy: workspaces → projects → tasks → subtasks. Its AI layer, called the AI Work Graph, analyzes project data to detect bottlenecks, flag dependency conflicts, and suggest timeline adjustments.

✅ Strongest at
🎯 Structured project management with clear dependencies
🎯 Team coordination across multiple parallel projects
🎯 AI-powered timeline analysis and risk alerts
🎯 Reporting and portfolio-level visibility
⚠️ Weaker at
⚠️ Flexible, freeform work — feels rigid for creative teams
⚠️ Visual customization and dashboard design
⚠️ Documentation — not a writing/notes tool
Pricing: Free plan (limited). Premium ~$11/user/month. Business ~$25/user/month. AI features require Business tier or above.
🏆 Best for: Marketing teams, operations teams, PMOs managing structured cross-functional projects with clear timelines and dependencies
🔴
Monday.com
Visual, flexible work management for visual thinkers

Monday.com is built around a highly customizable, spreadsheet-like interface that teams can adapt to almost any workflow. Its strength is visual flexibility — you can build almost any kind of board, dashboard, or tracker. Its AI features focus on automation (triggering actions based on status changes), content generation, and workflow suggestions.

✅ Strongest at
🎯 Visual dashboards and status tracking
🎯 Flexible customization — almost any workflow type
🎯 Automation of repetitive actions and notifications
🎯 CRM, sales, and operational use cases beyond just projects
⚠️ Weaker at
⚠️ Deep dependency management (not as powerful as Asana)
⚠️ Can become messy without strong governance
⚠️ AI features less mature than Asana's AI Work Graph
Pricing: Free plan (2 seats). Basic ~$10/user/month. Standard ~$14/user/month. Pro ~$24/user/month. AI included in Pro+.
🏆 Best for: Sales teams, agencies, operations teams, startups that need flexibility and visual clarity more than rigid structure
⚫
Notion AI
All-in-one workspace: docs, databases, and AI writing

Notion started as a documentation and note-taking tool but has evolved into a flexible workspace that combines writing, databases, and project tracking. Notion AI is embedded directly into the writing experience — it can summarize pages, generate content, extract action items, answer questions about your workspace, and help structure complex information.

✅ Strongest at
🎯 Documentation, wikis, and knowledge management
🎯 AI-assisted writing, summarization, and Q&A
🎯 Flexible databases for tracking almost anything
🎯 Combining project tracking and documentation in one place
⚠️ Weaker at
⚠️ Complex project dependency management
⚠️ Real-time project health monitoring and alerts
⚠️ Can require significant setup to work well
Pricing: Free plan (generous). Plus ~$10/user/month. Business ~$18/user/month. Notion AI is an add-on (~$10/user/month across all plans).
🏆 Best for: Startups, product teams, content teams, and any organization that prioritizes documentation alongside project tracking

📌 Article Summary

What to Remember

Asana excels at structured project management with complex dependencies and AI-driven project health monitoring. Best for operations, marketing, and PMO teams.

Monday.com excels at visual flexibility and workflow customization. Best for teams that need to adapt the tool to unusual or varied workflows, including non-project use cases like CRM.

Notion AI excels at combining documentation with project tracking. Best for teams where knowledge management is as important as task management.

Tool choice should match the team's primary need. No single tool is best for everyone — knowing the differences makes you more valuable in any business environment.
