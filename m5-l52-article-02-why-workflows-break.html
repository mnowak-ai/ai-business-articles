Why Workflows Break Down: The 5 Most Common Bottlenecks

Before AI can fix a workflow, you need to know how workflows fail. Every project delay, missed handoff, and chaotic launch traces back to one of five root causes. Learn to spot them — and you'll understand exactly what AI tools are designed to prevent.

Workflows Don't Break at Random

When a project falls behind schedule or something important gets dropped, it rarely happens for a mysterious reason. Workflow failures are predictable and patterned. The same five breakdowns appear again and again, across industries, company sizes, and team types. Knowing them in advance lets you spot the warning signs early — and gives you the vocabulary to fix them.

A delayed project isn't bad luck. It's almost always one of five things — and all five are preventable once you know what to look for.

What to Remember

Workflow failures are predictable. The same five bottlenecks — unclear ownership, missing dependencies, poor sequencing, resource conflicts, and communication gaps — account for the vast majority of project delays.

AI workflow tools like Asana's AI Work Graph are specifically designed to detect these five patterns — flagging unclear ownership, unmet dependencies, and resource overloads before they cascade into delays.

Being able to name the bottleneck type changes everything. "The project is late" is unsolvable. "Task 4 is blocked because it depends on Task 2 which has no owner" is immediately actionable.
